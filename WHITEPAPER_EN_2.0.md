# New Generation Fair Wealth Distribution System (Fair Wealth Distribution System)

## 1. Abstract
This whitepaper proposes a "decentralized, fair, and contribution-oriented" new generation organizational architecture and compensation algorithm system. The system aims to fundamentally solve the deep-rooted pain points in the traditional workplace: seniority-based promotion, middle-management exploitation, credit grabbing, and subjective bias. By introducing smart contracts, a dual-token economic model (Stable-CP and Equity-CP), multi-agent double-blind reviews, and Schelling point game mechanisms, this architecture strives to replace traditional human-ruled management with algorithms and cryptographic mechanisms, reshaping production relations and wealth distribution rules in the digital age.

## 2. Core Philosophies
* **Transparency over Authority:** All rules, task values (contribution points CP) and evaluation criteria (DoD) must be visible to all employees, breaking the "information black box" of management.
* **Contribution over Seniority:** Salary is determined by actual work intensity and task complexity. Seniority is only used as a "leverage" to amplify high output, not as a bargaining chip for mindless salary increases.
* **Algorithms over Human Bias:** Use algorithm mechanisms such as double-blind random extraction, label matching, etc. to eliminate worldly sophistication, cliques, and supervisor bias.

## 3. Key Mechanisms

### 3.1 The Dynamic Compensation Formula
Break the "dead salary + subjective bonus" model and adopt a dynamic calculation formula:
`Final monthly salary = basic living allowance + (work intensity × task complexity × unit price of contribution points) × experience leverage multiplier`
* **Anti-exploitation mechanism:** If older employees stop high-value output (extremely low intensity/complexity), their "experience lever" will fail and income will return to the basic level, completely blocking the exploitation of young executives.

### 3.2 Task Sub-contracting - Digital "Wangmiao Village Model"
* The task is clearly marked with a price (CP).
* The order taker can serve as a "micro CEO" and continue to subcontract tasks to the next level.
* Each node only needs to be responsible for the direct upstream and meet the acceptance standards of the upstream, which greatly reduces organizational communication costs.

### 3.3 Dual-Track Review & AI Persona Jury
In order to make up for the "collusion" and "technical debt" that may result from the pure subcontracting model, the system adopts dual-track parallel acceptance. For the biggest uncontrollable variable "people", the system introduces the **multi-agent jury** mechanism:
1. **Survival line (stakeholder acceptance):** The upstream contracting party is responsible for checking whether it can be used and solving business needs.
2. **Quality Line (AI Multi-Personality Jury Preliminary Trial):** For the deliverables, the system instantiates 3-5 AI Agents with different "personalities/expertise" (for example: "strict security geek", "extreme performance maniac", "picky UX experience officer"). Multiple AIs score independently, and if more than half reach a consensus, it will be passed or rejected directly.
3. **Ultimate line of defense (blind review by human experts):** Only when there are serious disagreements within the AI ​​jury, or when the contracting party initiates an "appeal" against the AI's verdict, will randomly selected human experts be triggered to conduct a double-blind review. Humans only deal with "marginal innovation" and "complex controversies."

### 3.4 The Evolution of Management
* **Middle managers:** The middle managers who purely "manage people" are eliminated. Transform into a "super contractor (architect)" and use hard core capabilities to break down strategic goals into executable CP tasks.
* **Product Manager:** Transform into a "funder", dig out needs and apply for CP budget from the system.
* **Project Manager:** Transformed into a "Scavenger/Lubricant". No longer a supervisor, but as an independent node taking on "coordination tasks" and earning CP by clearing cross-team blockers.

## 4. Compensation for Management & Special Roles

In the decentralized system, the traditional administrative affiliation relationship of "superior, middle-level, and subordinate" is completely broken and transformed into a contractual relationship between "the contract-issuing party (Party A) and the contract-receiving party (Party B)"**. The salaries of managers at all levels are no longer paid according to "rank", but are calculated according to their "ecological niche" in the value chain:

### 4.1 Middle Managers: From "Microphone" to "Super General Contractor/Architect"
* **Role change:** We no longer rely on poor information and distribution rights to exploit the bottom. They are architects with extremely high professional qualities.
* **Contribution calculation (earning price difference and structure fee): **
    * The top management issues a large strategic project (Epic) worth 10,000 CP. After the middle layer took over, it used its architectural capabilities to accurately disassemble it into 10 modules and subcontracted them to the bottom developers at a total price of 8,000 CP.
    * The remaining 2000 CP is the middle-level "architecture design fee" and "risk bearing fee".
    * **Restraint mechanism:** If the bottom layer fails to deliver, the middle layer, as the "general contractor", must bear joint and several liability (deducting CP or credit points). This forces the middle managers to understand technology and dismantling, and not over-squeeze the lower layers (otherwise no one will take orders or the delivery quality will be extremely poor).

### 4.2 Product Managers: From “Demand Maker” to “Internal Investor”
* **Role Change:** In the past, PM only had to hand over the requirements to R&D to complete the task. Now, the PM is the “funder”.
* **Contribution calculation (ROI investment return dividend):**
    *PM does not earn CP directly by "writing documents". Instead, they are the ones who spend the money. They apply for CP budgets from senior management and submit their needs to the task hall.
    * The PM's salary is absolutely bound to the **real business value (ROI)** after the demand goes online. If a feature built with 5,000 CP generates millions in revenue, the PM will receive a high profit share (translated into a massive amount of CP).
    * **Restraint mechanism:** If the PM randomly raises false requirements, resulting in no one using the project after it goes online, the PM will face "investment losses" and will not only lose dividends, but also reduce its future "budget application credit rating".

### 4.3 Project Managers: From “overseer” to “scavenger/bounty hunter”
* **Role change:** No longer an administrative manager holding a whip to push progress, but a service provider that solves cross-node friction.
* **Contribution calculation (service fee and bounty): **
    * When two subcontracting teams (such as front-end and back-end) have interface conflicts or progress obstruction, the system or the contracting party will issue a "coordination task" (such as a 300 CP bounty).
    * The PM takes over the task and ultimately promotes the delivery of the contract between both parties through communication, mobilizing resources, and resolving conflicts.
    * **Acceptance Criteria (DoD):** The obstruction is eliminated and joint debugging by both parties is passed. After achieving this, PM earns the service fee of 300 CP.

### 4.4 Reconstructing the Hierarchy of senior/middle/basic management relationships
* **Senior:** Controls the company's total capital and plays the role of **"central bank/macro regulator"**. They do not interfere with specific tasks and only guide the company's strategic direction by adjusting the "CP budget pool" of different business lines.
* **Middle (Middle):** Plays the role of **"large node/general contractor"**. Earn structure fees by relying on your hard-core dismantling capabilities and risk control capabilities.
* **Junior/Execution:** Acts as **"Micro Self-employed/Subcontractor"**. Earn task CP with excellent execution and professional skills.
* **Summary:** The traditional "superior and subordinate" has become an equal "contract network". There is no one who is the leader, only “who is the customer”.

### 4.5 Core Defense: Prevent subcontracting from degenerating into traditional exploitation (real relationships and evaluations of junior/intermediate/senior PMs)
**Pain point prediction:** If the senior PM subcontracts tasks to the junior PM, and the salary/evaluation of the junior PM is completely determined by the senior PM, the system will degenerate back to the traditional bureaucracy with "unequal distribution of wealth". Senior PMs become the new “exploiters.”
**System counter-mechanism (how to score and evaluate PMs at all levels):**

1. **Free Market Bidding, not forced assignment (Free Market Bidding):**
    * The senior PM cannot "order" the junior PM to do work. The senior PM must post the disassembled sub-tasks (such as writing a PRD for a certain module) to the "Task Hall" and mark the CP price.
    * If the senior PM bids too low (want to exploit), the junior PM can **refuse to take the order**. In a transparent market, prices must be at fair value, otherwise the senior PM's mission will be aborted and he will be subject to high-level accountability.
2. **Smart contract and objective DoD settlement, depriving subjective scoring rights (Smart Contract Settlement):**
    *The base CP income of a junior PM is not determined by the subjective rating of a senior PM.
    * Both parties establish an objective DoD when receiving orders (for example: PRD includes a complete data flow diagram and passes the technical feasibility review of the R&D team). As long as the junior PM reaches the DoD, the smart contract** automatically allocates CP from the senior PM's budget pool to the junior PM**. Senior PMs cannot be deducted.
3. **Business Value (ROI) Penetrating Dividends (ROI Traceability):**
    * This is the deadliest defensive weapon. The system will record the complete "subcontracting tree diagram".
    * When the product is launched and generates a profit of 1 million, the system will not give all the dividends to the senior PM for him to share. The system will **directly penetrate** according to the proportion on the smart contract and send the profit share due to the junior PM to the account of the junior PM. Senior PMs have no access to this money, so "grabbing for credit" is completely eliminated.
4. **PM also applies to "Blind Peer Review for PMs":**
    * Whether the requirements document written by the junior PM is good or not is not the final say of the senior PM.
    * The system will randomly select PMs from other business lines within the company (with corresponding capability tags) for double-blind review. This determines the junior PM's "quality score" and future "experience leverage multiplier."

## 5. In-depth discussion and future evolution (Advanced FAQ & Future Evolution)

### 5.1 Dialectical thinking: AI multi-personality jury vs. the boundary of human expert intervention

In the early stage, multiple AIs formed a jury to score together. If more than half of the AIs agree, it means that the deliverable meets mainstream standards and the next step (settlement) will be entered directly. This filters out 90% of routine tasks, completely eliminating the interference of human variables.
* **Tier 2: Human experts are responsible for "difference breaker and non-standard innovation":** When are humans needed?
    1. **AI disagrees:** When AIs with different personalities cannot reach a majority consensus (for example: the innovative AI gives 90 points, the conservative AI gives 20 points), it means that a complex edge scenario has been encountered.
    2. **Human appeal:** The contractor believed that AI could not understand its “extremely avant-garde architectural design” and actively applied for human intervention.
    At this time, the system will trigger the "Human Expert Schelling Point Consensus" in Section 5.5. The intervention of human experts is not for routine review, but to protect "edge innovation" and serve as the system's highest arbitration court.
* **The need for knowledge inheritance:** Human participation in the ultimate blind review is essentially a kind of **"mandatory cross-departmental technical exchange and knowledge sharing"** to prevent employees from becoming isolated islands that only interact with machines.

### 5.2 DoD and smart contracts: the “fund custody” model of paying first and doing work later
* **What is DoD? ** DoD (Definition of Done) is the **business standard** for acceptance (eg: interface delay <200ms). The **electronic smart contract** is the **underlying code logic** that implements these standards. DoD is the soul of the contract, and the contract is the executor of DoD.
* **Budget Lock-in (Escrow/Budget Lock-in):** The biggest pain point of traditional bidding is "work first, pay later", which can easily lead to late payment and disputes. This system adopts the **"Budget Pre-Freeze"** mode:
    * When releasing a task, the contractor must first apply to the system and **lock** the corresponding CP (funding).
    * The money is not in the account (not frozen), the smart contract cannot be created, and the task cannot be released.
    * Once the contractor completes the DoD (verified by AI and human dual-track), the smart contract will automatically unfreeze and transfer funds in an instant**. Completely eliminate "big pie" and "free prostitution".

Your deduction is very accurate! As AI takes over electronic flow and progress tracking, PM in the traditional sense will surely die.
* **Early stage (transitional state):** The project manager serves as a "scavenger" to promote process changes and resolve offline physical friction that AI cannot temporarily handle.

### 5.5 Anomaly Arbitration & Anti-Bribery
**Question:** Who pays the jury arbitration fees? How to plan? What if highly rated experts agree to cheat on arbitration fees?
**Mechanism design (jury rules and collusion prevention):**
1. **Loser Pays Principle:** Arbitration fees are not included** in the initial DoD budget. When arbitration is triggered (such as a dispute between the contractor and the contractor, or abnormal human expert ratings), both parties to the dispute must first pay an "arbitration deposit". After the jury's verdict, the deposit of the at-fault party (the losing party) will be deducted to pay the jury's arbitration fees; the winning party's deposit will be refunded in full. This greatly increases the cost of unreasonable and malicious scoring.
2. **Anti-expert collusion: Commit-and-Reveal Scheme: **
    * In order to prevent experts from communicating privately and "unanimously defrauding arbitration fees", the system adopts the "commitment-revelation" mechanism in cryptography.
    * When an expert votes, he submits an encrypted hash value (others cannot see what he voted for).
    *Only after all experts have submitted their submissions, the system will decrypt and publish the results. Because the experts are in an "information black box" when voting and do not know who the other jurors are, the physical conditions for collusion are completely severed.

2. **Anti-Collusion:**

If they are deceived by subcontractors and spend a lot of money to buy a bunch of useless code, and there is no commercial return (ROI) after the final product is launched, investors will bear huge losses. ## 7. Systematic critical reflection and limitations analysis (Critical Reflection & Solutions)
Looking at this system from an objective and critical perspective, although it is extremely logically consistent and fair, it has three fatal blind spots in practical implementation. The following are **cracking strategies** for these three blind spots:

### 7.1 The backlash of Goodhart's Law
* **Pain Point:** "When an indicator becomes a target, it is no longer a good indicator." If everything is settled on CP, employees will become extreme "egoists." No one will be willing to take on tasks that cannot be quantified (such as helping newcomers answer questions, refactoring bad ancestral code, and team building) because there is "no CP."
* **Cracking Strategies (Solutions):**
    1. **Retroactive Public Goods Funding (RPGF - Retroactive Public Goods Funding):** Drawing on the Web3 concept, the company draws 10% of the total profit every month and puts it into the "public fund pool". Employees can nominate colleagues who "silently contribute and help others", and all employees use "Quadratic Voting" to distribute the bonus. Make up for the lack of front-loaded pricing with back-loaded rewards.
    2. **Mentorship as Investment:** Helping newcomers answer questions is no longer a "free contribution". Senior employees can serve as “mentors” to newcomers. During the first 6 months of the new employee’s employment, for every CP earned by the new employee, the system will generate an additional 5% and send it to the tutor as a “career fee”. Turn “bringing new people on” into a long-term investment with high returns.
    3. **Tech Debt Bounties:** The reason for refactoring bad code is not that it cannot be quantified, but that the contracting parties (investors) are unwilling to pay. The system mandates that each business line must reserve 15% of the CP budget for "system reconstruction and performance optimization", and the architect issues a bounty.

### 7.2 Lack of Psychological Safety
* **Pain Point:** People are not purely rational economic beings. Although traditional organizations are exploitative, they also provide warmth, room for error, and a sense of belonging. Pure contract networks are extremely ruthless and can lead to extremely high mental stress and job burnout (“If you don’t take orders today, you won’t have food to eat tomorrow”).
* **Cracking Strategies (Solutions):**
    1. **Guild Mutual Insurance:** Allows super individuals to spontaneously form "Guilds". Guild members voluntarily pay 5% of their CP into the "guild treasury" every month. When members are sick, on vacation, or encounter a low period of "no orders", they can receive "unemployment/sickness benefits" from the treasury. Replace traditional company HR benefits with a decentralized mutual aid network.
    2. **Basic Life Security Base (UBI - Universal Basic Income):** Once again emphasize the `Basic Life Security Fund` in the formula. The system is by no means "zero base salary". The company must provide a base salary (not linked to CP) that is sufficient to sustain a decent life in the local area as a base for psychological safety. CP determines "wealth freedom", while basic salary determines "survival dignity".

### 7.3 Labor Law and Compliance Risk (Legal Compliance)
* **Pain Points:** The labor laws (including five insurances and one housing fund, minimum wage, dismissal compensation) in most countries around the world are based on the "employment relationship". Transforming all employees into "independent contractors (subcontractors)" involves huge compliance risks under the current legal framework, and can easily be judged as "fake outsourcing, real employment."
* **Cracking Strategies (Solutions):**
    1. **The Core-Cloud Model:**
        * **Core (core layer):** Sign a formal labor contract and pay five insurances and one fund. However, in terms of internal salary accounting, the traditional "fixed high salary + year-end bonus" is disassembled into "statutory basic salary + CP performance". It is legal and compliant externally and distributes according to work internally.
        * **Cloud:** True external freelancers, digital nomads. Completely adopt B2B (business-to-business) or B2C labor contract settlement, pure CP piece-rate, and avoid labor law risks.
    2. **DAO Legal Wrappers for DAOs:** In the future, you can use jurisdictions that are friendly to decentralized organizations (such as DAO LLC in Wyoming, USA, DAO entities in the Marshall Islands) to register to fundamentally establish the legality of "non-traditional employment relationships" at the legal level.

## 8. The ultimate red-blue confrontation: system-level fatal vulnerabilities and underlying protocol upgrades (Red Team Audit & Protocol Upgrades)
Based on the extreme stress test of the system (red and blue confrontation), we must face the core "fatal loopholes" of this architecture in the underlying logic, game theory, economics, law and technology, and propose the corresponding **underlying protocol upgrade (Patch)**:

### 8.1 Value quantification loophole: Is option CP a "big pie"? How to prevent the boss from running away?
* **Fatal attack:** Option CP sounds like traditional company options/stock dividends. What if the company goes bankrupt and the boss runs away (similar to migrant workers who are unpaid) and the options become waste paper? In the future of pure subcontracting, is option CP still necessary?
* **Protocol upgrade (Patch 1.0 - Smart contract interception and interest binding): **
    * **Essential difference from traditional options:** Traditional options are "the boss's promise", and the right of interpretation lies with the board of directors, who can dilute or fire you at any time. Option CP is **"the right to share accounts at the smart contract level"**. Every payment made by the user (such as through Stripe) will be directly transferred into the wallet of the option CP holder in proportion by the smart contract before entering the company's general account**. The boss cannot touch the money at all, nor can he "take the money and run away."
    * **Bankruptcy Liquidation Preferences:** If the project fails (no profit), the option does go to zero (this is a business risk). However, the system must ensure that: **Basic current CP (basic salary + basic piece rate) must be paid in full, and option CP only represents "excess earnings"**.
    * **Necessity of existence:** Under the absolute geographical arbitrage and subcontracting model, super individuals can easily become "pure mercenaries" (working for money, regardless of the life or death of the project). The existence of option CP is to transform "mercenaries" into "partners" and ensure the long-term vitality of the system.

### 8.2 The dilemma of objectification of subjective value: full-scenario oracles and free market pricing
* **Fatal attack 1 (who decides the proportion and the deadlock crisis): ** If it is completely left to P2P free bidding, will it become "whoever has the loudest voice (power) will get more"? If the two parties cannot reach an agreement and the demand is extremely time-sensitive, won't the system fall into a "deadlock"?
* **Fatal Attack 2 (Criticism of the "Benchmark Library" Solution): ** Other AIs have proposed "establishing CP pricing benchmark libraries for different industries to limit the pricing power of the contracting party." Is this possible?
* **Protocol Upgrade (Patch 2.0 - Hybrid Pricing, AMM Benchmark and Anti-deadlock Mechanism): **
    Here we must engage in extremely profound dialectical thinking. The "basic pricing + ex post value retrospection" proposed by other AIs is very correct (this coincides with our "liquid CP + option CP"). **However, "establishing an artificial pricing benchmark library" is a fatal "planned economy trap"** that will completely lock innovation. Our solution is as follows:
    1. **Reject the planned economy and introduce the AMM algorithm benchmark (Algorithmic Benchmark):** We do not set up an artificial "benchmark library", but introduce the automatic market maker (AMM) algorithm in DeFi. Based on historical transaction records, the system automatically calculates the "market fair average price" for tasks with specific tags (such as #React #High Concurrency) as a reference. The contractor can set the price freely, but if it deviates too much from the average price, the system will light up a red light to warn the contractor. This not only limits the monopoly of "loud voices", but also preserves the flexibility of the free market.
    2. **Crack the time-sensitive deadlock: Time-weighted Dutch auction (TWDA) and bonding curve pricing:** For situations where urgent tasks cannot be negotiated, once the task enters the "emergency countdown", the smart contract automatically takes over the pricing power. The increase ratio of CP is not artificially slapped on the head, but strictly determined by the Bonding Curve algorithm:
        * **Three major parameter locks:** When the contractor releases an emergency task, three values must be locked in the contract: initial quotation ($CP_{start}$), maximum budget ($CP_{max}$, which must be fully pledged in the contract), and absolute dead line ($T_{deadline}$).
        * **Exponential Premium Formula:** Over time, the current bounty amount increases exponentially. The logic of the formula is: $CP_{current} = CP_{start} + (CP_{max} - CP_{start}) \times (elapsed time / total countdown)^k$ ($k$ is an exponential factor, usually $k>1$ to form a sharp growth curve at the end).
        * **Game of Chicken:** This algorithm creates an excellent psychological game between contract recipients. Experts will stare at the rising CP and think: "Should I take the order now with a 20% premium, or wait until it rises to 50%? But if I continue to wait, I may be snatched away by others at any time!" This psychological pressure of "fear of missing out (FOMO)" will quickly match the transaction and break the deadlock in an instant. If it reaches $CP_{max}$ and there is still no one to take the order, the task will be automatically aborted, and the market will vote out the unreasonable demand with its feet.
    3. **Two-Part Tariff & Milestones:** For long-term innovation, completely abandon “full volume ex-ante pricing”.
